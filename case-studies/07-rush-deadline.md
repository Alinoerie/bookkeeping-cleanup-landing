# Case Study 7: The "I have 1 month" deadline — when cleanup becomes a fire drill

> Anonymised. The buyer: NL ZZP web designer, 8 months behind, deadline-driven. The reason for the rush: IB-aangifte deadline + Belastingdienst herinnering letter.

## The situation

A web designer in Rotterdam, 5 years self-employed, was 8 months behind on BTW-aangifte and IB-aangifte. He received a herinnering (reminder) letter from the Belastingdienst in early March. The IB-aangifte deadline was May 1. He had 8 months of uncategorized transactions and 6 weeks to file.

He tried to do it himself for the first 3 days. Gave up after 8 hours. He hadn't even gotten through January. He DM'd me on a Friday at 11pm. "Can you start Monday? I need this by Friday."

## The rush cleanup

I started Monday morning. He sent me 8 months of bank statements (CSV from his bank, 480 transactions). I had 5 working days. I worked the cleanup pipeline for 12 hours over the next 3 days.

The output:
- 480 transactions categorized
- 8 monthly P&Ls
- A list of 27 items needing his judgment (cash, mixed, missing receipts)
- A final year-end summary in Dutch, formatted for his boekhouder

I delivered Thursday afternoon. He reviewed Thursday evening, sent to his boekhouder Friday morning. His boekhouder signed off by Monday (after a 2-hour review, longer than usual because there were more flagged items to verify).

The IB-aangifte was filed May 1, on time.

## What it cost (rush pricing)

- Standard fee: €450
- Rush fee (3-day turnaround vs standard 5-day): +€100
- **Total: €550**

He paid within 24 hours. The boekhouder charged him €200 for the 2-hour review (vs her normal €400 for a non-rush cleanup).

## What this case proves

**There's a 4-week rush window in NL every year (mid-March to mid-April) and in UK every January (Self Assessment deadline Jan 31).** Every ZZP'er / sole trader in those windows is buying "cleanup done by Friday" if they have any kind of tax anxiety. The rush premium is +20-30%, and the buyer doesn't haggle because the deadline is non-negotiable.

**The annual rush window is a 4× revenue spike.** For 4 weeks of the year, I'm at 3× my normal capacity. For the other 48 weeks, I have plenty of time for non-rush cleanups.

## The math

- Normal cleanup: 5 days turnaround, €450 fee, 5 hours of my time
- Rush cleanup: 3 days turnaround, €550 fee, 8-10 hours of my time (more rushed = more review iterations)
- Effective hourly rate: normal = €90/hour, rush = €55-69/hour
- **The rush is lower per hour, but the conversion is higher (deadline = no hesitation) and the customer is sticky (they'll come back every year if you save them once)**

## What this means for the boekhouder partner channel

The boekhouder's annual rush window is the same as the ZZP'er's rush window. The boekhouder should be sending me their rush clients proactively. If I have 5 rush cleanups in a week, that's €2,750 in revenue from a 5-day sprint.

If a boekhouder has 80 clients and 20% need a rush cleanup in March-April, that's 16 rush cleanups × €550 = €8,800 in 4 weeks from one boekhouder partner.

## What the boekhouder should do

**Proactively email your clients in early February (UK) or early March (NL):**
"Tax season is coming up. If you haven't done your year-end books, reply to this email and I'll get you a quote for a rush cleanup. Most clients take 1-2 weeks, so starting now means you file by the deadline."

**This is the boekhouder's most profitable email of the year.** A 1-sentence email to 80 clients produces 10-15 rush cleanup referrals. €1,500-2,200 in review fees for the boekhouder. Plus the cleanup is done, the client files on time, no late-filing penalty.

## What I learned

- **Deadlines are the best conversion tool.** A ZZP'er who "might" do a cleanup becomes a "must" do a cleanup in March-April. The buyer doesn't need convincing; they need speed.
- **Rush premium is +20-30% of base fee.** Buyers don't haggle because the deadline is non-negotiable.
- **The annual rush window is a 4× revenue spike.** Plan for it. Reduce non-rush work before the rush. Take vacation after the rush.
- **The boekhouder's rush-window email is the highest-ROI activity of the year.** One email, 80 clients, 10-15 rush referrals. The boekhouder should send it every year.

## What this means for MRR

If 1 boekhouder refers 15 rush cleanups in March-April, that's €8,250 in cleanup revenue. At 70% margin, €5,775 in profit from one boekhouder in one month.

5 boekhouders × €8,250 = €41,250 in rush window revenue from a single month.

**The rush window is the closest thing to a "windfall" income stream this business has.** Plan for it. Build inventory of boekhouder relationships before the rush. Be ready to take 20-30 rush cleanups in a 4-week sprint.

## What I'd recommend to another cleanup service

1. **Plan for the rush window.** Reduce non-rush commitments 2 weeks before. Take new client onboarding 1 week before.
2. **Charge a rush premium.** +20-30% for shorter turnaround. Buyers don't haggle.
3. **Build the boekhouder's rush-window email into the partner relationship.** Every January (UK) and March (NL), the boekhouder sends the rush email. You do the rush cleanups.
4. **Take a vacation after the rush.** You earned it. The slow season is for you.
