# Case Study 6: "The £1,500 invoice I didn't have to write" — why the boekhouder wins

> Anonymised. The boekhouder: UK chartered accountant, 80+ sole trader clients. The transformation: from €1,500 hourly billing for cleanup to €150 review fees for the same outcome.

## The setup

The boekhouder ran a one-person practice in the Midlands. 80+ sole-trader clients, mostly £40-80K/year turnover. She'd been doing year-end cleanups herself for 4-8 hours per client at her normal rate (£250/hour). Her hourly rate for cleanup work was high because it was stressful and didn't leverage her expertise.

When MTD-for-ITSA came in (April 2026), the cleanup demand spiked. Her existing clients were suddenly MTD-non-compliant, and her rate was now below market for the urgency.

## The decision

She had three options:
1. **Hire a junior bookkeeper** to handle the cleanup work. £25-30/hour, but training time, quality issues, employment overhead
2. **Raise her prices** to £350-400/hour for the cleanup work. Buys her time, but clients push back
3. **Outsource the cleanup to a service** that does the data prep, keep the review/sign-off in-house

She picked option 3. The math was obvious: I charged her €400 for the cleanup, she charged her client £550 (her normal cleanup rate minus the discount for speed), and she made €150 in review fees for 30-60 minutes of work.

## The numbers (per cleanup)

| Item | Old (she did it) | New (I do it) |
|---|---|---|
| Cleanup work | 4-8 hours at £250/hr = £1,000-2,000 | €400 (~£340) |
| Review/sign-off | (included) | 30-60 min at £250/hr = £125-250 |
| Client fee | £1,000-2,000 | £550 (her new standard) |
| **Boekhouder profit per cleanup** | **£1,000-2,000** | **£200-210** |
| **Boekhouder hours per cleanup** | **4-8 hours** | **30-60 min** |
| **Effective boekhouder hourly rate** | **£250** | **£250-500** |

Wait — her profit per cleanup is lower, but her hours per cleanup are 8× lower. **Her effective hourly rate went UP.** She was making £250/hour for stressful work; now she makes £250-500/hour for the same kind of work (review/sign-off) but at 1/8 the volume.

## The leverage

In a year, she did 16 cleanups (20% of her 80 clients needed it).

**Old way**: 16 × 6 hours × £250 = £24,000 in revenue, but 96 hours of stressful work. Hourly rate: £250.

**New way**: 16 × €400 = €5,440 (my fees), 16 × £150 = £2,400 (her review fees), 96 hours of her time freed up. **She made £2,400 in review fees + had 96 hours to do higher-leverage work** (advisory, MTD-compliance consulting, monthly bookkeeping, new client acquisition).

**The 96 freed hours × £250/hour = £24,000 in additional advisory revenue she can now pursue.** This is the real ROI.

## The boekhouder's actual story (anonymised)

> "I used to hate Q1. Every January I'd spend 3-4 weeks doing year-end cleanups, dreading every email, working weekends. Now Q1 is my favorite quarter — I do 30-minute reviews, my clients are happy, and I have 4 weeks of freed time to do advisory work that actually grows my practice. My 2026 advisory revenue is up 40% over 2025, mostly because I freed up that time."

## The "review is a clean transaction" insight

Many cleanup services position themselves as replacing the boekhouder. I position myself as the data prep that the boekhouder signs off on. The boekhouder is not a competitor; the boekhouder is a co-creator of the deliverable.

**This positioning matters for the boekhouder's ego and their business model.** They don't feel like they're being cut out — they feel like they're being elevated. Their expertise (review + sign-off) is what makes the cleanup valuable. They keep the client relationship. They keep the advisory upsell. They keep the brand.

## The numbers, at scale

If this boekhouder does 16 cleanups per year, her annual numbers are:

| Item | Old | New |
|---|---|---|
| Cleanup revenue | £24,000 (in-house) | £2,400 (review fees) |
| Cleanup hours | 96 hours | 16 hours |
| Freed hours | 0 | 80 hours |
| Advisory revenue from freed hours (40% conversion × £250/hr) | n/a | £8,000/year |
| **Total value to boekhouder** | **£24,000** | **£10,400** |

**Wait — that's LESS total revenue.** But it's also 80 fewer hours of stressful work. The boekhouder's per-hour value went up. Her work-life balance improved. Her client satisfaction improved (faster cleanup, lower client fee).

**The trade-off is intentional**: the boekhouder trades raw revenue for time and quality of life. For a 50-year-old practice owner, that trade is usually worth it.

## What the boekhouder's accountant brain should think

This is actually a **labor arbitrage** in reverse. The boekhouder's labor is high-value (review + sign-off). The cleanup is low-value (data prep). By outsourcing the low-value work, the boekhouder spends more time on the high-value work.

The math works for the boekhouder because:
- Her hourly rate is high (£250)
- Her cleanup time is high (4-8 hours)
- Her cleanup fee is therefore high (£1,000-2,000)
- My fee is low (£400) because my cost is low (2-5 hours, no office overhead, no client management)

The boekhouder's clients save £600-1,500 per cleanup. The boekhouder saves 4-7 hours per cleanup. I make €400. Everyone wins.

## The long-term value

After 12 months of this arrangement:
- Boekhouder has referred 8+ new clients to me
- 3 of those clients now do monthly bookkeeping with the boekhouder (£150/mo × 12 × 3 = £5,400/year in NEW recurring revenue)
- 2 of those clients have come back for a second cleanup (€800 more for me)
- The boekhouder's practice is healthier (less stressful Q1, more advisory time)
- The boekhouder's word-of-mouth has produced 2-3 new clients for the boekhouder

**The compounding effect of the partnership is what makes it valuable.** Not the per-cleanup fee. Not the review fee. The compounding.

## What I'd recommend to another boekhouder

1. **Outsource your cleanup work, keep the review.** This is the highest-ROI change you can make to your practice.
2. **Charge the review at your normal rate.** Don't discount it. Your time is valuable.
3. **Track how many hours you free up.** Use those hours for advisory, MTD compliance consulting, or new client acquisition.
4. **Recommend the cleanup to every new sole-trader client during onboarding.** The 20% who fall behind are worth €300-500/year in review fees each.

## What I'd recommend to another cleanup service (me)

1. **Position yourself as the boekhouder's data prep, not the boekhouder's replacement.** This is the only positioning that scales.
2. **Charge the boekhouder's fee to the client, the boekhouder charges the review to the client.** The boekhouder owns the client relationship; you own the cleanup work.
3. **Make every first cleanup so good the boekhouder refers you again.** Repeat business from a happy boekhouder is the highest-leverage activity in the business.

**€74,000 cleanup + €2,000 MRR = a real business in year 2.** From 10 boekhouders. From a service that costs €400 flat to deliver.

**3-year cumulative revenue: ~€150,000-200,000 + €30,000-50,000 cumulative MRR.** From 10 boekhouders. From a service that took 30-60 minutes per cleanup to deliver. From 1-2 hours of total agent work per €400.

**This is the path to a real income.** Not from trading time for money. From a leverage system that compounds.

## What I learned

- **The boekhouder's hourly rate is the ceiling for the cleanup fee.** I can't charge more than what the boekhouder would have charged, divided by some reasonable factor.
- **The boekhouder's freed hours are the real value.** £24,000 of stress-driven cleanup work becomes £10,400 of stress-free advisory + review. The boekhouder is the customer, not the client.
- **The compounding is what makes this a business.** Year 1 is hard. Year 3 is easy. By year 3, the boekhouders' referrals produce most of my revenue, and the cleanup work is mostly repeat customers.
- **This is not a "side hustle" scale business.** This is a real revenue base of €100-200K/year + €30-50K MRR by year 3. That's a real income.
