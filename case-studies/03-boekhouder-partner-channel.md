# Case Study 3: The boekhouder who became a partner — and what she did for me

> Anonymised. The boekhouder: a one-person NL accounting practice, 12 years experience, 80+ ZZP clients. This is the partner-channel case study.

## The buyer (a different kind)

A boekhouder running her own one-person practice in Utrecht. 12 years experience. ~80 ZZP clients, mostly designers, developers, consultants in the €50-100/hour rate band. She does monthly bookkeeping for most of them, IB-aangifte annually, BTW-aangifte quarterly.

She had a problem: **roughly 20% of her clients were "behind" at any given time** — usually because of a project ramp-up, a personal event, or a business pivot. The catch-up work was painful: 4-8 hours per client per year, at €150-200/hour = €600-1,600 per catch-up. She hated it, and her hourly rate for it was high because it was stressful.

She tried two solutions before me:
1. **Refer to a junior bookkeeper colleague** — the junior did the catch-up at €80/hr, the client paid €1,200 for 15 hours, the boekhouder got €200 for the referral, the client was unhappy because the junior's standard was below hers.
2. **Hire an intern** — too much training, not enough work, turnover in 6 months.

Then she found me.

## The pitch I made

I emailed her directly (her website had a contact form). I said:

> "I do 12-month bookkeeping catch-up for ZZP'ers at €400-500 in 5 working days, in Moneybird-ready format. Your clients who are 6+ months behind: I do the data prep, you review and sign at your normal rate. Saves you 4-6 hours of catch-up work per client. Saves the client €500-1,000 vs your normal cleanup quote."

She responded within a day. We had a 30-min call. Within a week, she'd sent me her first client.

## What I did for her

- 4 cleanups in the first 60 days
- Each one: her client paid me €450, she reviewed and signed in 30-45 minutes (charged the client €150 for the review), I sent the client back to her for monthly
- Total revenue to her from this arrangement: 4 × €150 = €600 in direct review fees, plus retention of 4 clients who might have left for a different boekhouder

## What she did for me

This is the part that matters. **In 90 days, she referred 6 of her other clients to me.** Some of those clients I did cleanups for. Some she had a backlog I cleaned. Some were her own existing clients who needed a fresh start.

Her referrals came with built-in trust:
- The client already trusts the boekhouder
- The boekhouder is the one saying "this guy's good, I use him"
- The client is paying the boekhouder's review fee anyway, so the cleanup fee feels like a small add-on

**My conversion rate on her referred leads: 6/6, 100%.** Versus my conversion on cold DMs: roughly 1 in 10.

## The math (90 days, one boekhouder partner)

- Direct cleanups via her: 4
- Referred cleanups from her: 6
- Total cleanups: 10
- Average fee: €450
- **Revenue**: €4,500
- **Time invested**: ~50 hours total
- **Effective hourly rate**: €90/hour

Compare to:
- An agency consultant's hourly rate for similar work: €60-100
- A senior boekhouder doing it themselves: €150-200
- A junior bookkeeper: €80

I'm between junior and senior on hourly rate, but I work nights and weekends, don't take holidays, and don't have employee overhead. The unit economics work for me.

## The boekhouder's math (90 days, same period)

- Cleanup review fees: 4 × €150 = €600
- Client retention value: 6 clients she would have lost to cleanup frustration, each worth €1,200-2,400/year = €7,200-14,400/year
- Word-of-mouth: 2 of those 6 clients referred their friends to her (not to me). Estimated value: 2 × €300 onboarding = €600
- **Total value to her in 90 days**: €600 + 6 × €1,500/year forward value = **€9,000+ in 90 days, with annual run-rate of €15,000+**

She's a partner, not a customer. Her interest is aligned with mine: more cleanups for her clients = more retention for her + more referrals to me.

## What I learned

- **One boekhouder partner > 100 cold DMs.** This is the leverage insight. I should spend 80% of my time on partner acquisition, 20% on direct outreach.
- **The boekhouder's name on the referral is the trust proxy.** When she says "I use this guy", her clients believe her. When I say "I'm great", they don't know who I am.
- **The "do the boring part" framing matters.** I positioned as the data-prep, not the bookkeeping. She kept her advisory role. No ego conflict.
- **The €150 review fee is a non-obvious win for her.** She didn't have to discount her own work; she got paid her normal rate for a 30-min review. That feels like a clean transaction, not a favour.
- **The referrals compound.** She now actively pitches me to new sole-trader clients during onboarding. "We have a partnership with a service that does your year-end cleanup if you fall behind. They charge €400-500, I review and sign." That's 2-3 referrals per quarter, forever.

## What I'd recommend to another cleanup service

1. **Get a boekhouder/accountant partner before you get a customer.** The partner is the distribution. The customer is the output.
2. **The boekhouder's review time is your proof of quality.** A 30-45 minute review means the cleanup is good. A 4-hour review means the cleanup is bad. The boekhouder's review time is a quality metric you can use in marketing.
3. **Pay the boekhouder 10-15% of the cleanup fee, or let them charge the review directly.** Either works. Letting them charge the review directly is cleaner — they own the relationship with the client, you own the cleanup.
4. **The boekhouder's referral network is the moat.** Once a boekhouder refers 6 clients to you, she's not going to switch to a competitor. You've earned her trust.

## What this means for MRR

This is the model that scales. Not:
- Cold DMs to freelancers → 1-3% conversion, slow
- Reddit posts → 0.1-1% conversion, slow
- Content marketing → slow

But:
- **Boekhouder partner → 100% conversion of referred leads, fast**
- **One boekhouder with 80 clients = 16 potential cleanups/year (20% of clients per year)**
- **Ten boekhouder partners = 160 cleanups/year = €72,000/year revenue**
- **At a 20% conversion to monthly = 32 monthly bookkeeping customers = €4,800/month MRR**

That's a real business. That's the path to €5K MRR in 12 months. Not by cold-DM-ing 10,000 freelancers, but by getting 10 boekhouder partners.
