# Case Study 5: "Why I almost didn't take the cleanup" — the trust gap and how to close it

> Anonymised. The buyer: a UK freelance designer, £35K/yr, 11 months behind on Self Assessment. The reason he almost said no: fear of HMRC penalties. The reason he said yes: the trial.

## The buyer's situation

A freelance designer in Bristol, 4 years self-employed. £35K/year. Uses FreeAgent. Was 11 months behind on Self Assessment and dreading it. Had received a "we haven't received your return" letter from HMRC. Wasn't sure if it was a warning or a notice or just a routine letter.

He'd heard about the cleanup service from a Reddit thread. He DM'd me. He was interested. Then he went silent for 3 days.

## Why he almost said no

When I followed up, he said: "I'm worried your categorization will be wrong and I'll get a bigger fine than the £100 late-filing penalty. My accountant quoted me £280 to do the cleanup, and even though that's a lot, at least it's a person who knows what they're doing."

This is the **trust gap**. The buyer knows the problem. They know I might solve it. But they don't trust me enough to give me their financial data. The risk of a misstep feels bigger than the benefit of saving £50-200.

## What I did

I offered him the **first 100 transactions free** as a trial. No payment, no commitment. I categorized 100 of his transactions and sent back:
- The categorized CSV
- A short summary: "I categorized 100 transactions, here's what I noticed, here's what your accountant should review"
- A direct comparison: "Here's how my categorization compares to what FreeAgent's auto-categorization would do. They're similar but I'm more specific on NL/UK tax codes."

The trial took 2 hours. I sent the output. He reviewed it with his accountant (over a 15-minute call). The accountant said: "This is what I'd do. Sign off."

**Within 24 hours, he paid the £400 invoice.**

## What changed his mind

Three things:

1. **The free trial removed the financial risk.** He didn't have to commit €400 to a stranger. He could validate quality first.
2. **The accountant's sign-off removed the trust risk.** His accountant looked at my work and said "yes, this is what I would do." Now my work had a trusted third-party endorsement.
3. **The categorization was specific to UK tax codes.** FreeAgent's auto-categorization is generic. My output was specific to HMRC's expected categories. The accountant could see the difference.

## What this case proves

**The "first 100 free" trial isn't a loss leader. It's the trust mechanism.** Most buyers in this market are not "I'll try anything." They're "I need to be sure before I give someone my financial data." The trial gives them the verification path they need.

The conversion rate from trial to paid customer (in this case) was 100%. In a larger sample, I'd expect 40-60% conversion — much higher than the 1-10% I'd get from cold outreach.

## What the boekhouder learns

**Position the trial as "your accountant will see this and approve it before you pay."** That's the trust bridge. The boekhouder is the gatekeeper. The trial gives the boekhouder a way to validate without risk.

When I approach a boekhouder, my pitch is: "Send me one client who has a backlog. I deliver a free 100-transaction sample. Your review is the proof. If you don't like it, you walk. If you do, the next cleanup from this client is a paid €400 job and you take the review fee."

## What I would have done differently

I waited 3 days to follow up. That was too long. **The follow-up should be 24 hours.** Most buyers' interest decays fast, especially for a service that requires sharing financial data. A 24-hour follow-up would have closed the deal 2 days faster and saved 1 customer from a competitor who might have replied first.
